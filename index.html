What We do

Research Area I: Improving Work through Software and Data

There are three main areas of focus within this topic: using natural language processing to improve qualitative data analysis and requirements engineering, software engineering research methods, and software engineering education. Read more about any research area on Dr. Barcomb's personal research website.

Using natural language processing to improve qualitative data analysis and requirements engineering

Collaborators associated with this research area include Dr. Andreas Kaufmann and Dr. Feng Chen.

Examining qualitative data analysis in software engineering research

External collaborators associated with this research area include Dr. Dirk Riehle, Dr. Andreas Kaufmann, Dr. Nikolay Harutyunyan, and Elçin Yenişen Yavuz.

Software engineering education

Our collaborators include Dr. Emily Marasco.

Research Area II: Human Factors in Software Development

This line of research focuses on the people and processes involved in the creation of software. There are three main areas of research under this theme, in addition to single papers exploring other ideas. The main areas are: open source software contributors and communities, diversity and inclusion, and component and tool selection. Read more about a research area on Dr. Barcomb's personal research website.

Open source software contributors and communities

Current collaborators in this area of research include Elçin Yenişen Yavuz, Mehmet Ali Erol, Dr. Roisin Lyons, and Dr. Klaas-Jan Stol.

Diversity and inclusion

Recent collaborators in this area include Dr. Alexander Serebrenik and Dr. Sebastian Baltes.

Component and tool selection

In this work we collaborate with Dr. Gias Uddin and Dr. Lorenzo De Carli.
